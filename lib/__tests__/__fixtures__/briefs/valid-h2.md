# Acme Corp Website Brief

A quick overview of what we need.

## Home

The home page is the front door of the site. We want a hero with the tagline "Build better websites faster" followed by a short subhead that explains what the product is in two sentences. Under that there should be three feature cards with icons, each describing one of our main value props: speed, flexibility, and support. Each feature card should have an outlined monochromatic icon, a short headline, and a two-to-three sentence description. Below the cards, include a testimonials strip with two or three customer quotes. Each testimonial should carry a small circular avatar, the verbatim quote, the customer name, and the role plus company on a single line below the name. Close with a final call-to-action section pointing people to the free trial. Keep the tone confident but warm, and avoid marketing jargon wherever we can. The header should stay fixed on scroll and include links to About, Pricing, Docs, and a "Start free trial" button rendered in the primary color. The footer should have four columns organized as Product, Company, Resources, and Legal, each with four to six links. Include the company's San Francisco address, links to Twitter / LinkedIn / GitHub, and a short copyright line beneath the columns. The home page is the first impression, so aim for a balanced layout that is neither too dense nor too airy. Think Stripe meets Linear in aesthetic terms, with generous whitespace and a clear visual hierarchy that leads the eye from the hero through the value props to the CTA. Feature-card icons should be outlined (not filled) and monochromatic, and the testimonials should be visually subtle — no oversized quotation marks, no heavy card backgrounds. Use our primary color sparingly; let the content do the work and reserve color for the CTAs and the occasional accent. The page must be fully responsive down to 375 pixels wide, with the feature cards stacking into a single column on mobile and the testimonials becoming a horizontally scrollable strip. Keyboard navigation and visible focus rings are table-stakes — every interactive element gets a focus outline that meets AA contrast. Page load target is under 1.2 seconds on a mid-range phone on a cable connection; that means no carousels that block paint, no decorative images above 150 kB, and no third-party scripts on the landing path other than our own analytics bundle. If we need to add social-proof logos between the hero and the value-props block, they should be grayscale and sized uniformly so the row reads as a single visual unit rather than a collage.

## About

Tell the story of the company — why we started, who we serve, what we believe. Start with a one-line mission statement near the top. Include a team section with five headshots and short bios. Mention that we're a remote-first team spanning four time zones. Close with a short "What's next" paragraph hinting at upcoming product work. The tone should be personal and specific; avoid cliches about "revolutionizing" anything.

## Pricing

Three tiers: Starter, Pro, Enterprise. Starter is free, Pro is $29/month, Enterprise is custom pricing. Emphasize Pro as the recommended choice. Include a comparison table listing what's in each tier — seats, support SLAs, integrations. Add an FAQ below the pricing cards. Include questions about cancellation, billing, trial length, and data export.

## Integrations

List every integration we support. Group them into categories: CRM, Analytics, Communication, Dev Tools. Each integration should have a logo, short description, and a "Learn more" link. Include a search box at the top.

## Contact

Simple form with name, email, company, and message. Also show an office address and a support email. Keep it short.
